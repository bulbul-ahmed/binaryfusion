FIRE RECRUITMENT AUSTRALIA

**H1: The e-learning centre behind one of Australia's toughest recruitment processes.**
A complete training and assessment platform — web, iOS, and Android — that takes firefighter candidates from first login to final interview.

*(Snapshot bar)*
**Industry** Recruitment & Training · **Platforms** Web app, iOS app, Android app · **Stack** Laravel, Flutter, AWS, Firebase **[CONFIRM stack list]** · **Engagement** Multi-year, ongoing **[FILL dates]**

**H2: The situation**

Firefighter recruitment in Australia is unforgiving: each intake draws thousands of applicants for a handful of places, and candidates face aptitude testing, psychometric profiling, physical assessments, group exercises, and panel interviews — often across months. Fire Recruitment Australia coaches candidates through that gauntlet. To do it at scale, they needed more than a course library: they needed a platform that could *train, test, and track* every candidate, on whatever device the candidate had, with the rigor of the real selection process built in.

**H2: What we built**

**A guided journey, not a content dump.** Every candidate works through a structured program — Start Here, Aptitude Engine, Personality Profiler, Physical Aptitude, Application Builder, Group Assessment, Interview Formula — with per-module progress bars, self-set deadlines, and an overall journey score on the dashboard. The destination is explicit: a "Dream Job Secured" milestone candidates mark when they get the offer.

*(Full-width screenshot: dashboard with module cards and progress ring. Caption: Each module tracks its own completion; the journey ring rolls everything up.)*

**An examination engine built for high stakes.** Timed mock exams mirror the real aptitude tests, with question-level statistics — correct, incorrect, unanswered, and score — so candidates train on their weaknesses instead of re-reading their strengths. Module completion gates a final exam, with the full checklist (training series, practice exams, group sessions, 1-1 calls) tracked on one screen.

**A cognitive-training arcade.** The platform's "Amplify for Cognify" suite turns decision-speed and accuracy training into games — with daily streaks, total play time, per-game accuracy analytics, and anonymous leaderboards so candidates compete without exposure. Gamification here isn't decoration; quick, correct decisions are literally what the fire service tests for.

*(Two-up screenshots: Aptitude Engine checklist + Amplify for Cognify leaderboard. Caption: Streaks, play time, and per-game accuracy keep practice daily instead of last-minute.)*

**Everything else a candidate needs in one place.** A video lesson library with structured playlists. Personality profiling with exam statistics. A resume and application builder shaped to what fire services actually screen for. Booking flows for group sessions and 1-1 coaching calls. Free and premium resource tiers. All of it synchronized across the web portal and the iOS and Android apps, so a candidate can drill aptitude questions on a lunch break and review the session at home.

**H2: Engineering notes**

Three problems shaped the architecture. *Progress integrity:* completion state feeds module gating, deadlines, and the journey score, so progress events are the system's source of truth rather than a cosmetic percentage. *Tiered access:* free, premium, and program-specific content share one catalog with role- and entitlement-based access control, so the business can repackage offerings without re-uploading content. *Three clients, one truth:* web, iOS, and Android consume the same API, which keeps exam timing, scoring, and progress identical no matter where a candidate trains.

**H2: The outcome**

The platform is Fire Recruitment Australia's product — it runs the business, not a corner of it. Candidates train, test, book coaching, and build applications in one system, and the team ships new modules with us as the program evolves. **[FILL: candidates trained, completion or success metrics, app ratings — numbers to request from FRA.]**

# PAGE: CASE STUDY — T-RANZ `/work/t-ranz`

T-RANZ RENTALS

**H1: The operations platform behind a 107-vehicle rental fleet.**
A self-drive vehicle rental system — customer booking, fleet management, and admin apps — built around how an Australian rental company actually runs, because nothing off the shelf could.

*(Snapshot bar)*
**Industry** Transportation & Vehicle Rental · **Platforms** Customer web app, admin platform, admin iOS & Android apps · **Stack** Laravel, Flutter, MySQL, AWS **[CONFIRM]** · **Engagement** August 2023 — ongoing

**H2: The situation**

T-Ranz rents vans, utes, and minibuses across Sydney — Toyota Hiace commuters, one- and two-tonne LWBs, Uber Eats delivery vehicles. Rental software exists, but none of it could model their reality: seasonal rate structures, charter bookings, relocation charges, toll handling, pre-authorization card lifecycles, and a fleet where every vehicle has its own service schedule and status. The workarounds were costing staff time and creating errors. They asked for a system shaped like their business.

**H2: What we built**

**A reservation pipeline, end to end.** Quotes become reservations become charters, with overdue tracking, courtesy reservations, closed-booking history, and a live dashboard of today's pickups and dropoffs. Staff see new reservations, totals, and overdues the moment they log in — on desktop or on the admin apps we shipped for iOS and Android.

**Fleet management with real depth.** Every vehicle carries its make, model, category, type, color, registration, service kilometres, and a status lifecycle — available, on sale, sold, damaged — with bulk actions across the fleet. Categories and subcategories model everything from 12-seater minibuses to delivery hatchbacks.

*(Full-width screenshot: Vehicles table. Caption: 107 vehicles, each with service tracking and a status lifecycle staff update in one click.)*

**A pricing engine that understands seasons.** Seasons, rates, rate structures, and rate modules let the business price the same vehicle differently by period and product — with date blockers, lead-time blockers, and charter booking blockers preventing the bookings that shouldn't exist.

**The financial plumbing.** Payment scheduling, pre-authorization card expiry tracking, transaction reconciliation with a pending-reconciliation queue, deposits, extra charges, relocation charges, fuel and toll integration, and insurance handling — the details that decide whether a rental month balances.

**A report builder instead of report requests.** The Advance Custom Report module lets staff compose their own reports: filter by location, vehicle, customer, status, and date range, then select from more than 90 columns across customers, reservations, pickups, returns, vehicles, scheduled payments, transactions, and formula fields. The reports the owner used to ask a developer for, staff now build themselves.

*(Full-width screenshot: Advance Custom Report. Caption: Nine data domains, 90+ selectable columns, saved as named reports.)*

**Run like a real system.** Role manager, user administration, activity logs, automated backups, bulk email promotions, blogs and testimonials for the customer site, and a message board for internal coordination.

**H2: Engineering notes**

The hard part of rental software is that *everything touches pricing*: seasons, blockers, extras, relocations, and tolls all converge on one quoted number, so the rate engine is built as composable rules rather than special cases — which is why the business can add a new charge type without a developer. The second hard part is trust in the data: activity logs and reconciliation queues exist because a rental company's disputes are settled by records, not memories.

**H2: The outcome**
